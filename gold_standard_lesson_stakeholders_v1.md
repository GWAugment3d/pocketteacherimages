# Stakeholders – Gold Standard Lesson

## Core Idea
A stakeholder is any individual or group that is affected by, or has an interest in, a business and its decisions.

Businesses must consider stakeholders because their actions can impact profits, reputation, and long-term success.

---

## Types of Stakeholders

Stakeholders can be split into two main groups:

### Internal stakeholders
These are people inside the business such as employees, managers, and owners.

They are directly involved in running the business and depend on it for income and job security.

### External stakeholders
These are people outside the business such as customers, suppliers, government, and the local community.

They are not involved in daily operations but are still affected by business decisions.

---

## Stakeholder Objectives

Different stakeholders want different things:

### Customers
- Good quality products
- Value for money
- Reliable service  
If these are not met, they will switch to competitors.

### Employees
- Job security
- Good pay
- Safe working conditions  
If unhappy, motivation and productivity fall.

### Shareholders
- High profits
- Dividends
- Rising share price  
They want strong financial performance.

### Suppliers
- Regular orders
- On-time payment  
Late payment damages relationships.

### Government
- Businesses follow laws
- Pay taxes
- Create jobs  
Failure can lead to fines or legal action.

### Local community
- Jobs
- Low pollution
- Positive local impact  
Poor behaviour damages reputation.

---

## Stakeholder Conflict

Stakeholders often want different things.

For example:
- Employees want higher wages
- Shareholders want higher profits  

Increasing wages raises costs and reduces profit.

Another example:
- Customers want low prices
- Shareholders want high profits  

Lower prices reduce profit margins.

---

## Stakeholder Influence

Stakeholders can influence businesses in different ways:

- Employees → strike action  
- Customers → boycott  
- Shareholders → voting at AGM  
- Local community → protests  

The more power a stakeholder has, the more the business must respond.

---

## Real Example

A company like Tesco must balance multiple stakeholders.

Customers want low prices, employees want higher wages, and shareholders want higher profits.

If Tesco lowers prices, customers benefit but profits may fall.

If Tesco cuts costs, profits increase but employees may become demotivated.

---

## Application

Imagine a business replaces workers with machines.

Employees lose jobs (negative impact), shareholders gain higher profits (positive impact), and customers may benefit from lower prices.

One decision affects stakeholders differently.

---

## Exam Link

In a 6 or 9 marker, you should:
- Identify a stakeholder
- Explain their objective
- Analyse the impact of a business decision

Example:
"Employees want job security. If a business cuts jobs to reduce costs, employees may become demotivated. As a result, productivity may fall."
